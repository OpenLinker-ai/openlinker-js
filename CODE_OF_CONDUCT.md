# Code of Conduct

OpenLinker projects should be useful, respectful, and focused on the work.

## Expected Behavior

- Be direct and constructive.
- Assume public comments will be read by contributors with different levels of
  context and experience.
- Keep criticism technical, specific, and actionable.
- Respect maintainers' decisions about scope, security, and release timing.
- Avoid sharing private information, secrets, credentials, or live customer
  data in issues, discussions, pull requests, or logs.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, personal attacks, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing another person's private information.
- Posting real secrets, tokens, customer data, or exploit details in public
  channels.
- Repeatedly derailing technical discussions after maintainers ask to move on.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns to the maintainers through the project's published
support channel. Report vulnerabilities through the process in
[SECURITY.md](./SECURITY.md), not through public issues.

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove content and may limit participation when
needed to keep the project safe and productive.
